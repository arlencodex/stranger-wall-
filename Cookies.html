Cookies

Cookies are small text files websites store on your device to remember your visit and improve your experience — here's how they work and what we do with them.

Cookies can store data such as login details, language preferences, and website settings. When a website remembers your username or keeps items in your shopping cart, it is using cookies.

Although cookies make websites more convenient and personalized, they can raise privacy concerns when too much information about your browsing habits is collected without your knowledge.

Types of Cookies

Session cookies are temporary and disappear when you close your browser. They handle things like keeping you logged in during a single visit.

Persistent cookies stay on your device after you close your browser and remember your preferences for future visits.

Third-party cookies are created by external companies — usually advertisers — to track your activity across different websites and serve targeted ads.

How Cookies Affect Your Privacy

Cookies can affect your privacy because they may track your online behavior. Websites and advertisers can use this information to understand your interests and show targeted advertisements — sometimes collecting data about the pages you visit, how long you stay, and what you click.

The impact depends heavily on which type of cookies a site uses. Session cookies are largely harmless. Third-party tracking cookies are the ones most worth watching.

Managing Your Cookies

You are always in control. To protect your privacy you can manage cookie settings in your browser, delete cookies regularly, or block third-party cookies entirely. Private browsing mode also reduces the amount of information stored during your session.

  • Session cookies — only last for your browsing session, gone when you close the tab.
  • Persistent cookies — remain after you close your browser, remember your preferences.
  • Third-party cookies — often used for tracking across sites, blockable in all major browsers.
  • You are in control — manage them via browser settings or use private mode anytime.

Stranger Wall & Cookies

We only use a minimal, temporary session cookie to remember your preference — like whether you've seen the welcome message. It expires the moment you close your browser.

We do not use third-party cookies, tracking pixels, or analytics cookies. Your anonymity is our priority. You can delete or block cookies at any time through your browser settings — the wall will still work, though you might see the welcome message more often.
